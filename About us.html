ABOUT US

ISIB's mission is to aid rising or current IB students by providing resources of explanation, advice and sharing experiences. It is purposed at easing the journey to the DP from the point of view of IBDP students in years 1 and 2 in a somewhat informal manner; though since the IB is ever-changing, it's worth to note that at some points certain aspects of this website, if not all of it may contain invalid information as it becomes outdated.

What is ISIB?

ISIB is a website made by 3 IB students studying at different schools around the globe experiencing the IB in various ways. Derived from the need of more accessible help, ISIB aims at filling in a space that IB guides, booklets, and presentations leave uncovered. To do this, ISIB includes simplified explanations of assessments and some tips and trick to guide you through the IB, in addition to accounts of personal experiences to demonstrate what the IB can be like and how despite it being a singular, internationally recognized system, it still has differences here and there that often have big impacts one your learning experience and academic life.

What is IB (DP)?

The International Baccalaureate has 4 programs (as of the making of this website; 2022) the Primary Years Program (PYP, 3-12yrs old), Middle Years Program (MYP, 11-16 yrs old), Diploma Programme (DP, 16-18yrs) and Careers Program (CP, 18-21 yrs old). ISIB will only focus on and detail the Diploma Programme, also known as IBDP, which is taken the last two years of highschool. “The Diploma Programme (DP) curriculum is made up of six subject groups and the DP core, comprising theory of knowledge (TOK), creativity, activity, service (CAS) and the extended essay. Through the Diploma Programme (DP) core, students reflect on the nature of knowledge, complete independent research and undertake a project that often involves community service.” 1

Individual details

Ayesha Ballen (she/her)

Ayesha is American but did all her highschool studies in Brazil, more specifically, the American School of Rio de Janeiro (Escola Americana do Rio de Janeiro). Having family from Nicaragua and Colombia and living in central Latinamerica, she grew up bilingual and learned Portuguese and French through school, hence the Lang B French SL As an international student, her parents sought to keep a consistent program so she could study without major issues, since the IB provided such, she always studied in schools with most if not all programmes offered by the IB. Having had a clear, set-out path like this one prepared her for the last two years that were the DP. “I didn't really have much of a choice whether i wanted to do the IB or not, but for any students wondering if they should take it, I’d say assess your current conditions and from those derive a reasonable answer.”

The subjects I took:

  • Language B French SL
  • Biology SL
  • Math Analysis and Approaches SL
  • Language A, English: Language and Literature HL
  • History HL
  • Visual Arts HL
